Step-by-step instructions for reading a primary research article

Cong Wang

Here’s the link to the original post.

Procedures

  • Skip the abstract and avoid any bias from the authors.

  • Identify the BIG question: What problem is this ENTIRE FIELD trying to solve?

  • Summarize the background in five sentecens or less.

    • What has been done before in this field to answer the BIG question?
    • What are the limitations of that work?
    • What, according to the authors, needs to be done next?

  • Identify the SPECIFIC QUESTIONS

    • What exactly are the authors trying to answer with their research?
    • Write their questions down.

  • Identify the approach

    • What are the authors going to do to answer the SPECIFIC QUESTIONS?

  • Now read the methods section. Draw a diagram for each experiment, showing exactly what the authors did.

  • Read the results section. Write one or more paragraph to summarize the results for each experiment, each figure, and each table. Write down what they are but not what they mean.

    • For good papers, majority of the results are summarized in the figures and tables.
    • Difficulty might arises at this point due to lack of background (collection of trees).

  • Do the results answer the SPECIFIC QUESTIONS? What do you think they mean?

  • Read the conclusion/discussion/interpretation section.

    • What do the authors think the results mean?
    • Do you agree with them?
    • Come you come up with an alternative way of interpreting them?
    • Do the authors identify any weaknesses in their own study?
    • Do you see any that the authors missed?
    • What do they propose to do as a next step? Do you agree with that?

  • Go back to the beginning and read the abstract.

  • What do other researchers say about this paper?

    • Who are the experts in this particular field?
    • Do they have criticisms of the study that you have not thought of, or do they generally support it?

  • (Optinal) Go to the literature section and see what other papers the authors cited.
